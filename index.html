Research

I am broadly interested in building NLP models robust to variations in data such as domain and efficient in parameters. I consider interpreting models a crucial step in achieving this since only when we find out what ML models learn we can guide them to learn better.

I am also interested in promoting inclusivity in NLP, especially by curating resources for under-represented languages, preferably in a manner that requires minimal human intervention.

I have applied my research interests across NLP applications such as Sentiment Analysis, Reading Comprehension, Question Generation, and WER Estimation. Representative papers are highlighted .

Model Interpretability

I researched and identified the behavior of CNNs, especially what they learn and what they don't, to propose SCARN model built on recurrent and convolutional structures for effective text classification[1]. Since Curriculum Learning by its nature involves a lot of hyperparameters and design choices, I worked on analyzing CL's effectiveness to understand best when it can be useful. I used attention visualizations to explain that CL works by breaking down a harder problem into multiple easy sub problems. The in-depth exploration of CL for Sentiment Analysis culminated in my Master's Thesis[2].

Robust Natural Language Processing

How can we build NLP models which will be robust to variations in data such as domain. My research at Samsung focused on Domain Adaptation. Domain Adaptation helps build models that scale across domains. We have proposed gated convolutional architectures for this problem. The gated mechanism can filter out domain-specific information [2], and further refined this idea to present a Sequential Domain Adaptation framework [1]. I am also interested in exploring Robust NLP in building models that are impervious to noise and are unbiased.

Efficient Natural Language Processing

Lately, perforamnce improvements in NLP tasks, are made based on increasing parameters rather than efficiently training just a single model. Hence, I am interested in achiving efficiency in NLP models. This can be achieved in multple ways. Curricululm Learning proposes one way of achieving thsi by introducing an ordering to the training but using the smae model 2]. Other ways to acheive parameter efficiency could be by proposing special loss functions [1] or layers [4]. I have also proposed parameter less decay layers which simulate learned functions but are parameter less themselves [3].

Resources for under-represented languages

Modern machine learning algorithms rely heavily on data, and this reliance has adverse effects in resource-scarce settings. I improved two pre-existing lexicon-based resources in Telugu[3,4,6]. We also created Hindi resources for Question Answering and to aid educational applications divided into academic grades[5].

While resource creation for scarce languages is essential, hand-curating datasets can be time and labor intensive. Hence, I gravitated towards automated resource creation. We developed a solution to exploit the massive emoji-rich social media presence of Indian languages to automate the creation of multilingual sentiment analysis datasets[1]. We also made a linguistically informed rule-based system that relies on semantic roles identified via dependency parsing to convert Hindi sentences into questions to facilitate HindiQA[2].
